A boiler can be working perfectly and the house can still be cold. If radiators are cold at the bottom, if some rooms never get warm, if the system is noisy or slow to heat, the problem is normally in the system rather than the boiler itself.

Sludge, poor balancing, undersized radiators and ancient controls all cost you money quietly, every single day the heating is on. Fixing them is usually far cheaper than replacing a boiler that was never the problem.

  • Smart controls and thermostats (Nest, Hive and similar)
  • Thermostatic radiator valves fitted

  • Full central heating system installation

Smart controls

A smart thermostat is one of the few upgrades that genuinely pays for itself. Heating the house only when someone's actually in it, room by room, is where the savings come from.

It's not right for everyone, and it won't rescue a system that's full of sludge. But fitted to a system that's in good order, it's a real improvement rather than a gadget.

Central heating & upgrades FAQs

Why are my radiators cold at the bottom?

Almost always sludge. Corrosion debris settles in the bottom of the radiator and blocks the flow. A power flush plus a magnetic filter usually resolves it.

What is a power flush?

A machine pushes cleaning chemicals through the system at high flow to strip out sludge and corrosion. It restores circulation, improves efficiency and protects a new boiler.

Are smart thermostats worth it?

On a system that's in good order, generally yes. On a system full of sludge, fix the system first. A smart control can't fix bad circulation.
